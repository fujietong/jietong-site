Dr. Jietong Fu

Lyric Tenor • Educator • Performer

About Me

Dr. Jietong Fu is a lyric tenor specializing in Italian bel canto and Mozart repertoire. Praised for his warm lyric tone and expressive musicality, he is based in the Baltimore–Washington, D.C. area, where he maintains an active performing and teaching career.

He has recently performed Nemorino in L’elisir d’amore with Portland Opera, and created the role of Kamegoro in Shizue: An American Story. His operatic repertoire also includes Tamino (Die Zauberflöte), Ernesto (Don Pasquale), and Alfred (Die Fledermaus).

In concert, Dr. Fu has appeared as tenor soloist in major works including Mozart’s Requiem, Handel’s Messiah, and Rachmaninoff’s Vespers. He has appeared at major venues across Asia and the United States, with performances in Beijing, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Hong Kong, and Washington, D.C., including the National Centre for the Performing Arts in Beijing and the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts.

In addition to his performing career, Dr. Fu is an experienced voice teacher and coach. He has taught at Xiamen University and Texas Tech University, and served as a Lyric Diction Tutor at the Peabody Conservatory, working with singers in Italian, German, French, and English repertoire.

Dr. Fu holds a Doctor of Musical Arts from Texas Tech University and a Graduate Performance Diploma from the Peabody Conservatory.
